01

The Question & the Decision

Why this analysis exists

What characteristics drive churn (subscription cancellation) among Pulseboard Pro-plan accounts, viewed against the 2025 active Pro base?

Churn = subscriptions.status = 'canceled' where accounts.plan = 'pro'. Matches the canonical churn_rate in metrics.yml.

Decision this supports

Where to focus Pro retention effort — onboarding, pricing/packaging, or CS outreach — or to conclude no intervention is warranted because 2025 "Pro churn" is subscription rationalization rather than customer loss.

03

Hypotheses

Criteria pre-registered before deeper analysis

H1 · Price tier drives churn

Primary

Higher-priced Pro subscriptions churn more.

Confirms ifCancel rate rises monotonically with tier; $199 ≥ 1.5× the lowest non-zero tier.
Refutes ifCancel rates flat across tiers within noise.

H2 · Early-life tenure

Primary

$199 churn concentrates early in the subscription lifecycle.

Confirms ifMedian canceled-$199 tenure well below active tenure; clusters in first ~90 days.
Refutes ifCanceled and active tenure distributions overlap; no early concentration.

H3 · Engagement decline

Alternative

Low / declining product activity precedes churn. Testable only within event coverage (2025-03-07 → 06-06); else inconclusive.

Confirms ifCanceled accounts show materially lower pre-cancel event intensity than active peers.
Refutes ifNo gap — or canceled accounts more active (the "engagement paradox").

H4 · Support burden

Alternative

More / unresolved support tickets precede churn.

Confirms ifCanceled accounts show higher ticket volume or open-rate pre-cancel.
Refutes ifNo separation from retained accounts.

H0 · No driver / rationalization

Null

Canceled subs are indistinguishable from retained ones beyond noise, and most "churn" is accounts dropping one subscription while staying active. Live data already shows 5/5 of 2025 cancels retained an active sub — deep analysis must quantify this and test whether price/tenure survive once rationalization is separated out.

05

Known Issues Applied

Carried forward from learning/known_issues.md
  • plan is lowercase — filter WHERE plan = 'pro', never 'Pro'.
  • events cover only ~90 days (2025-03-07 → 06-06) — H3 restricted to in-coverage cancels, else inconclusive.
  • SQL rank-tie bug — any rank-sum test uses average-rank (min+max)/2, not RANK().
  • Small n (7 cancels, 5 in 2025) — descriptive full-population reporting; confidence capped Medium (price/tenure), Low (industry/support/engagement).
