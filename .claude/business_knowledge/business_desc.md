Pulseboard — Company Context
Pulseboard is a mid-market B2B analytics platform (Series A, ~50 paying customers). Think "Looker-lite" for teams that have outgrown spreadsheets but aren't ready for a full BI consultant. Customers connect their operational data and self-serve dashboards, reports, and embedded analytics from there.
Who the customers are
Typical Pulseboard customer is a 10–200 person company. Current book of business spans seven industries: eCommerce, MarTech, SaaS, HealthTech, FinTech, CyberSecurity, and EdTech. No industry is more than ~20% of the base.
Inside each customer account you'll find three user roles:

Admins run the account — billing, invites, permissions. They log in sporadically.
Analysts are the power users. They build dashboards and write queries. If analysts go quiet, that's a meaningful signal.
Viewers consume dashboards, usually on Monday mornings. High viewer-to-analyst ratio is healthy; all viewers and no analysts is a warning sign.

How the business is packaged
Three plans: a free tier for trial usage, Pro ($49/mo), and Enterprise ($99/mo). Most customers are on annual contracts, which means churn shows up quietly — users disengage long before the renewal conversation.
What "good" looks like
Healthy accounts show regular analyst activity, a mix of event types (not just logins), low error/timeout rates in product events, and low support ticket volume relative to their size. The analytics agent should treat sudden drops in analyst activity or spikes in error events as leading indicators worth surfacing, even when revenue metrics still look fine.
